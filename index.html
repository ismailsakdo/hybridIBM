The Silent Crisis in Our Buildings

Poor Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) is not just an inconvenience; it's a critical public health and economic issue. This section details the severe consequences of outdated, reactive building management systems that fail to protect occupants and assets effectively.

⚠

Health & Economic Burden

Poor IAQ leads to Sick Building Syndrome (SBS) and Building-Related Illnesses (BRI), costing over $5 billion annually in the US alone from lost productivity and absenteeism.

📈

Reactive Management

Traditional systems only react to failures after they occur. This leads to costly emergency repairs, prolonged downtime, and extended exposure of occupants to unhealthy conditions.

🔒

Disconnected Data Silos

Crucial data from sensors, maintenance logs, and occupant feedback are trapped in separate systems, making a holistic understanding of building health impossible.

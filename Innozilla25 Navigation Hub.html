üèÜ Competition Submission Guide

Follow these steps for the Innovation Competition submission (must be in the Innovation Competition group):

  1. Click SUBMIT YOUR INNOVATION (choose your category: Professional / Student).
  2. Click ADD SUBMISSION.
  3. Critical: You must submit your information LINE BY LINE (press ENTER after each line) into the provided submission box. Your final submission must consist of 8 lines:
    • Line 1: PROJECT TITLE (Long)
    • Line 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT (maximum of 100 words)
    • Line 3: INNOVATIVE SOLUTION (maximum of 100 words)
    • Line 4: INNOVATION PHOTO (Google Drive Share Link - Ensure viewable!)
    • Line 5: INNOVATION VIDEO (YouTube Unlisted Link)
    • Line 6: PROJECT ABSTRACT (Upload PDF/DOC file)
    • Line 7: YOUR NAME / TEAM MEMBERS (IF ANY) ‚Äì INCLUDING TITLE
    • Line 8: YOUR EMAIL
  4. When complete, click SAVE CHANGES. You can Edit/Delete before the submission deadline.
üé§ Speaker Profile Submission

Follow these steps (must be in the Speaker group):

  1. Click the SPEAKER SUBMISSION button.
  2. Click SUBMIT.
  3. Submit your profile information LINE BY LINE (press ENTER after each line). Your submission must consist of 5 lines:
    • Line 1: NAME WITH TITLE
    • Line 2: ORGANIZATION
    • Line 3: YOUR TITLE OF PRESENTATION
    • Line 4: YOUR BIBLIOGRAPHY (Brief Bio)
    • Line 5: PROFESSIONAL PHOTO (Google Drive Share Link - Ensure viewable!)
  4. Click SAVE CHANGES.

‚öñÔ∏è Jury System Usage & Assessment
  1. Access the Grading Interface: Navigate to the Innovation Competition activity within the course to view assigned submissions.
  2. (Head Jury Only) Submission Allocation:

    Click SWITCH TO NEXT PHASE (if required) and then ALLOCATE SUBMISSION to assign specific entries to respective jury members based on expertise/field.

  3. Evaluation: Click on the submission title to review the project details (the 8 lines of text, photo link, video link).
  4. Entering Marks: Click the Grade/View Mark button to access the rubric and input your scores. You are mandated to EVALUATE MINIMUM OF 10 SUBMISSIONS.
  5. On-Site Re-Assessment: During the event, you can click RE-ASSESS to adjust marks if needed after meeting the participant. Always click SAVE AND CONTINUE to record your updated evaluation.
  6. (Head Jury Only) Finalizing Grades:

    Once all marks are in, the Head Jury must click RE-CALCULATE GRADES and then SWITCH TO THE NEXT PHASE and CONTINUE to finalize the competition results for the category.

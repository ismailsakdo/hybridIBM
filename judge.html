Sport Innovation Challenge 2025: Evaluation Rubric

Category: Professional (Academia, Industry, TVET, College)

Section A: Core Assessment (Weighted Criteria)
Criteria & Weight 0 (Poor) 1 (Fair) 2 (Good) 3 (Excellent) Score = (Criteria/100) x Z (score) Total Mark = Score/3 x 100
1. Novelty (20%)
Judge Checklist: IP & Publication Status
 Not unique. Minimal originality and heavily derivative of existing solutions. No IP filing. No publication. Some elements differentiate it from existing ideas, but lack of significant innovation. Copyright filed/granted or Conference proceedings/Book chapter published. Moderately unique. Offers some novel features, but still somewhat similar to existing concepts. Patent/ID filed/granted or Scopus indexed journal publication. Highly unique. Demonstrates exceptional creativity, significantly divergent from existing solutions. Utility Patent/ID granted or ISI (WOS) indexed journal publication.
2. Applicability & Impact towards SDG (15%)
Judge Checklist: Problem/Solution & Prototype
 The invention's practical use in sports is unclear. Negligible impact on SDG. No prototype.

Poor quality patent search report (lens.org). 		Partially effective resolution with limited sports sector impact. Minimal positive impact on selected SDG. Beta version prototype available.

Basic patent search report with some relevant information. 		Moderately effective resolution with noticeable impact on the sports industry/community. Moderate positive impact on several SDG. Minimal Viable Product (MVP) prototype available.

Adequate patent search report covering essential aspects. 		Highly effective resolution with significant impact on sports performance/health. Outstanding positive impact, contributing significantly to SDG achievement. Prototype ready for market fit.

High-quality patent search report with thorough analysis.
3. Technology Readiness Level (TRL) (15%)
Judge Checklist: Research Grants, Collaboration, Field Validation
 TRL 1-2 (Basic principle or Concept formulation). Technology research; pure science begins translation to R&D.

No research grant or industry collaboration (No MoA/MoU/LoI/RCA). 		TRL 3-4 (Experimental Proof of Concept or Lab Validation). Start active R&D; ready to begin bridge for tech transition. Prototype shows some functionality but has issues.

PRGS grant or LoI present. 		TRL 5-7 (Validation/Demonstration in real environment). Ready to enter system development. Prototype is satisfactory in functionality and usability.

TED 1, MyLAB, or Industrial grant/MoU present. 		TRL 7-9 (System complete/qualified or proven). Ready for full-rate operation; compliant and test complete. Prototype excels in functionality, usability, and reliability.

TED 2, FRGS, or MoA present.
4. Commercial Potential (30%)
Judge Checklist: Income, Spin-Off, Marketing, Packaging
 Lack of understanding of the sports market or ineffective strategy. No plan or action taken towards commercialisation. Fail to engage the audience. Demonstrates some understanding of the target market and outlines a basic strategy. Some progress made (e.g., prototype development), but still in early stages. Clear understanding of the target sports market, competitive landscape, and effective strategy for market penetration. Significant progress made with clear milestones achieved. Robust market analysis, innovative business model, and strategic approach that positions the invention for significant market success. Ready for market launch/already commercialised, with successful revenue generation and scalability.
5. Presentation (20%)
Judge Checklist: Poster, Video (5-8 min), Demonstration
 Minimal creativity. Provides some basic information but lacks depth or clarity, leaving the audience with unanswered questions. Demonstrates some creative elements that capture attention but lacks consistency and organization, requiring further clarification. Presentation showcases outstanding creativity, utilising novel approaches to captivate and inspire the audience. Presents complex concepts with clarity and depth, and offers valuable insights that leave the audience well-informed and educated.
